What the Model Found

There is a moderate positive correlation (r = 0.489) between a team’s share of players in the top‑5 European leagues and how far it advanced in Qatar 2022. FIFA ranking is a slightly stronger individual predictor (r = -0.501), with the negative sign reflecting that a lower rank number means a stronger team.

Together the two predictors explain 28.9% of the variance in stage reached (R² = 0.289). The regression coefficient on pct_in_top5 is 1.118: a team with 100% of players in top‑5 leagues is predicted to reach 1.12 stages further than a team with 0%, holding FIFA ranking constant. Each one‑place drop in FIFA ranking shifts the predicted stage by -0.025.

Limitations

  • Small sample (n=32). A handful of upsets — Saudi Arabia beating Argentina in the group stage, Morocco reaching the semi-finals — can shift the regression line noticeably at this sample size.
  • Ordinal response. Stage reached is an ordered category, not a continuous quantity. The gap between Group Stage and Round of 16 is not necessarily the same “distance” as between Semifinal and Finalist, yet the model treats them equally.
  • Multicollinearity. pct_in_top5 and FIFA ranking are themselves correlated (r = -0.694), making it harder to isolate the individual contribution of each predictor.
  • Omitted variables. Injuries, tournament draw luck, home‑continent advantage (Qatar, Morocco), squad chemistry, and coaching quality all influence results but are absent from the model.
  • Playing time not captured. Being registered at a top‑5 club does not mean playing time. A bench player at PSG is counted the same as a starter at Bayern.

Takeaway

The two linear algebra tools — correlation matrices (built via mean‑centering and matrix multiplication) and least‑squares regression (solved exactly through the normal equations) — give a clean quantitative picture of the relationship between club‑league environment and international success.

Top‑5 league experience is meaningfully associated with World Cup success, but it explains only about a third of the variation. The remaining two‑thirds is a reminder that tournament football is genuinely unpredictable — and that is precisely what makes it worth watching.
